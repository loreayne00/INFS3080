Pattern of the Week

Tipsy Shells Scarf

This week's PotW is the Tipsy Shells Scarf, using Caron Cakes yarn. I chose Pistachio for my colorway, but there are many delightful combinations to chose from. This pattern lends itself well to the natural color changes in Caron Cakes and it is an easy one skein wonder!


Abbreviations:
Ch – Chain
Sc – Single Crochet
Dc – Double Crochet
St – Stitch
Rep – repeat/repetition

Materials
US H/8 5.0 mm Crochet Hook (or appropriate hook for chosen yarn)
1 ball Caron Cakes – Colorway: Pistachio

Measurements
59” (150 cm) x 12” (31.5 cm) without fringe.



Instructions:
Ch 40. (Or a multiple of 12 plus 4, for desired width.)

Foundation Row: 1 Sc in 2nd ch from hook and in every ch across row, turn.

Row 1: Ch 3 (counts as first dc here and throughout), skip first Sc, 1 dc in each of the next 3 sts, *skip next 2 sts, 5 dc in next st, ch 2, skip next 4 sts, 1 dc in each of next 5 sts, repeat from * across ending last rep with 1 dc in each of last 4 sts, turn.

Row 2: Ch 3, skip first dc, 1 dc in each of the next 3 dc, *skip next ch-2 space, 5 dc in first dc of next shell, ch 2, skip next 4 dc of shell, 1 dc in each of the next 5 dc, repeat from * across, ending last rep with 1 dc in each of the next 3 dc, 1 dc in top of turning ch, turn.

Rep row 2 for pattern until desired length is achieved (appx. 59’’/ 150cm). Fasten off. Weave in ends.

Finishing: Cut 3 pieces of yarn for fringe 12” in length, fold in half and loop through the end of the scarf, starting in a corner. Repeat across the ends of the scarf six more times or until fringe is as thick as desired.
